QUANTUM MACHINE LEARNING NOTES
==============================

Quantum neural networks (QNNs) are a emerging paradigm that combines quantum 
computing with machine learning. Unlike classical neural networks that use 
binary bits, QNNs leverage quantum bits (qubits) which can exist in 
superposition states.

Key Concepts:
- Variational Quantum Circuits (VQC)
- Quantum Feature Maps
- Parameterized Quantum Circuits (PQC)
- Measurement-based quantum computing

The main advantage of QNNs is the potential for quantum advantage in 
high-dimensional feature spaces. However, current NISQ devices have 
significant limitations.

Some math:
- |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩ where |α|² + |β|² = 1
- The cost function C(θ) = ⟨ψ(θ)|H|ψ(θ)⟩
- Gradient: ∂C/∂θ using parameter shift rule

Quantum convolutional neural networks (QCNNs) are interesting because
they avoid the barren plateau problem to some extent.

Applications: drug discovery, quantum chemistry, optimization problems.

Limitations: noise, decoherence, limited qubit count, error rates.
