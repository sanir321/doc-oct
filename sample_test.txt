Machine Learning for Natural Language Processing: A Comprehensive Review

Abstract
This paper presents a comprehensive review of machine learning techniques applied to natural language processing (NLP). We examine traditional approaches, deep learning methods, and recent transformer-based architectures.

Introduction
Natural language processing has undergone significant transformation with the advent of machine learning. From rule-based systems to neural networks, the field has evolved rapidly. This review covers the key developments and methodologies.

Methodology
We conducted a systematic review of literature from 2018 to 2024, focusing on major NLP tasks including text classification, named entity recognition, machine translation, and question answering. The review includes both supervised and unsupervised learning approaches.

Results
Our analysis shows that transformer-based models have achieved state-of-the-art performance across most NLP benchmarks. BERT and its variants demonstrated particular strength in understanding contextual relationships.

Conclusion
Machine learning continues to drive innovation in NLP. Future work should focus on efficiency, interpretability, and handling low-resource languages.

Keywords: machine learning, natural language processing, deep learning, transformers, BERT
